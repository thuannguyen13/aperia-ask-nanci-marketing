An open model we own, reached by a fast, low-risk path

Model selection & hosting strategy for the merchant / ISO conversational-analytics agents · Prepared by Bill Biddy, SVP Technology · July 2026

We will launch quickly on a rented, managed model to prove the experience, then move the production workload onto an open model we host and control ourselves. This gives us speed now and ownership, predictable cost, and full data control later — without changing the product in front of the customer.

The approach

Start on a managed cloud model to validate the merchant and ISO experience in days, with no infrastructure to build. Once the experience is proven, migrate the production workload to an open model that runs entirely inside Aperia’s own environment. The customer-facing product stays the same throughout; only the engine behind it changes, and each change is a redeploy rather than a rebuild.

Why Qwen3 8B is the recommended open model

Across a weighted evaluation of small (≤15B) open models — scored on tool-calling, reasoning, efficiency and enterprise fit — Qwen3 8B ranks first on capability. It has strong tool/function calling for our MCP + DAB orchestration, runs efficiently on a single GPU, and ships under a clean, commercial-friendly Apache 2.0 license. Because we self-host it, the weights run inside our own boundary — no customer data leaves and there is no callback to the vendor — which is how we contain model-provenance concerns for a payments platform. Adoption remains subject to a provenance governance sign-off.

What “open model” means — and how it lets us own the IP

A closed model (like a typical hosted AI API) is a black box we can only rent: the provider holds the actual model, we send data in and get answers back, and we pay per call for as long as we use it. We never possess the thing doing the work.

An open model ships its full weights — the trained model file itself — under a license (Apache 2.0) that lets us download, run, modify and deploy it freely. Once we self-host those weights, the working model becomes an asset Aperia owns and controls: it runs on our infrastructure, inside our security boundary, on our schedule. We can version it, fine-tune it on our own data to build proprietary domain expertise, and keep running it with no per-token bill and no dependence on a vendor’s pricing, availability or terms. That is how an open model turns the LLM itself into intellectual property we own rather than a service we rent.

What this delivers

  • Speed & low risk to start — a working ISO pilot in days on a managed model, zero infrastructure.
  • Ownership & independence — the production model is ours to run, tune and control; no vendor lock-in.
  • Predictable economics — self-hosting replaces a per-token meter with fixed, reserved cost as volume grows.
  • Data control & compliance — everything runs inside Aperia’s boundary, fit for a PCI / payments platform.

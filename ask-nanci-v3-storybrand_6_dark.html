The honest truth

Managing a merchant portfolio has never been more complex.

Stitching together information from five different sources to make one decision is not a sustainable workflow. It is a ceiling on how fast your operation can move and how confidently it can grow.

A flagged merchant means five tabs before you even know what you are looking at. Risk in one place, case notes in another, transaction history somewhere else. Your analysts are good at their jobs. They are just spending too much time on the wrong part of it.

Most operations have accepted this as the cost of running a complex portfolio. It is not. It is a solvable problem, and the solution is not more screens.

The operational backbone

Work queues are where the old model
is most entrenched. And where this proves itself.

Risk queues. Case queues. Exception lists. Alert inboxes. This is where operational work lives. Ask Nanci doesn't replace the queues. It changes what it feels like to work one.

01
Prioritization is manual

The user scans and decides what to work next every single time. That judgment should come from the system.

02
Context is fragmented

Working one case means opening five tabs and assembling context by hand. Ask Nanci brings it all to you.

03
Patterns are invisible

Twelve cases caused by the same issue look like twelve unrelated tickets. Ask Nanci sees the connection.

04
Handoffs are clunky

Escalating means leaving the queue, writing a note somewhere else, and hoping someone sees it in time.

05
The queue is passive

It shows what's there. It doesn't tell you what's important, what's related, or what to do next. Ask Nanci does.
